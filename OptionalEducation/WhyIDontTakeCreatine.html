Why I Don't Take Creatine
Ben Cliff Simmons
June 19, 2026, 11:42a.m. ET
HALIFAX, NS - My partner does take creatine. But I, speaking for myself, have not done so. Although many people take creatine and it is very popular -- and only getting more popular -- taking it is not something I have done.

On the one hand, this is because, despite my partner having it in the house, I don't remember where they put it and they change its location semi-regularly, although I don't think that has anything to do with me -- that is, I don't think they are trying to keep me from knowing where the creatine is stored. On the other hand, I don't have a curiosity to take creatine.

On the one hand, this is because the word reminds me of another word, which I can't recall, but which, through this association, makes creatine seem like something else, which I know why I don't want to take; however, I don't remember what it is that I associate with creatine, only that I don't want to take it and that, by association, I do not have a curiosity regarding creatine. On the other hand, I do not yet have a regular physical exercise regimen, such that I might benefit from creatine use, meaning that any creatine use would be exploratory or recreational and, although I am familiar with the benefits of taking creatine when performing highly energy-intensive activities, I am less familiar with any recreational, therapeutic, or spiritual applications.

That being said, based on what I know about the mechanism according to which creatine functions, there is no reason to imagine that it shouldn't have some property beyond enhancing the performance of high-intensity exercises; however, I am not aware of any such alternative benefits and, as stated previously, lack the curiosity to explore further, especially given that, as stated previously, it is currently inconclusive whether creatine has exploratory applications or whether it does not. Until we can say for certain whether -- and how -- creatine impacts the realm of inner experience, such that it would be productive of realizable benefits not only in the external, that is, the physicalized dimension, but also internally, such that it might result in beneficial effects in activities of a lower, or different, intensity, such as practices geared towards exploring the inner reaches of the realms of interior experience, then curiosity about the alternative applications of creatine will have to produce results for another person, or preferably an institution interested in the public good, such that the results of their investigations could, at some later date, possibly influence those of us who, for reasons of our own, are not currently drawn to it, but who hold out for the possibility of some day developing a genuine, felt sense of curiosity that might give rise to a desire to personally experience creatine use for ourselves.

For now, it is not that time, but the future promises to hold many surprises. This might be one.
Ben Cliff Simmons is a nutritional consultant and part-time life coach based in Halifax, Nova Scotia. He is currently between exercise regimens and actively exploring his sexuality.
