Fact Society: Film Watch
His Game of Lives: Exclusive Interview w/ Serena Biosfera
Justin Goldberg
August 5, 2022, 8:54a.m. ET
Cuernavaca, MX - His Game of Lives is one of the feature length films hitting the big screen at the Puerto Vallarta Independent Film Festival later this year. It represents a labor of love for a cast and crew of largely newcomers to the film industry.

This is director Eric Parton's first feature length film and is a collaboration between writers and producers from both Mexico and the United States.

We spoke to Serena Biosfera who is the lead writer for His Game of Lives to hear an insider perspective of the film and its production.


Interview with Serena Biosfera
Image courtesy of Getty Images.
Justin Goldberg: In your own words, what is the story His Game of Lives is looking to tell?
Serena Biosfera: The movie is a departure from what audiences are used to seeing. We wanted to create a story that was relatable while still being firmly rooted in an abstract world. The main characters, Liewen Ludo and Alexis Mentiros, are opposing forces in the nightclub scene. One of them is the kind of person you bump into at the start of the night who's mellow and friendly while the other is more like the person you find pouring out the drinks and buying rounds for strangers. It creates such a strange energy, an almost mysterious aura when they meet that I think audiences will be left speechless by the subtext. [Laughs]

What were the largest challenges in filming such a complex production?
Serena Biosfera: Apart from COVID which halted our production for almost 12 months? Probably the filming location. Right from its inception, we knew that this was a story that could only happen in New York, it just made sense. However, due to visa and licensing issues almost all the filming was done right here [gestures] in Cuernavaca. Capturing the vibe of New York City and teleporting it over here took its toll on all the crew. [Laughs]

That's awesome. The title, His Game of Lives, seems to suggest something very ominous may be on the horizon; how did you settle on the title?
Serena Biosfera: Yes! Social manipulation plays a large role in the movie, especially since so much of it is set in a nightclub. Plus, near the end of the movie it's revealed that the driving force behind everything that has been going wrong is a sort of metaphysical being called 'His', so at that point in writing the screenplay the title was a no brainer! [Laughs]

Going back to the 'New York' aspect of filming, what steps did you take to portray New York while filming in a separate location?
Serena Biosfera: It was all done with practical effects and carefully selected filming locations. I have never been to New York specifically, but I was raised in Puebla which is a big city not too far from here, so I took a lot of inspiration from that. Ultimately, the story we're telling with His Game of Lives could have happened anywhere, so we don't try to lean too heavily into a specific location. Hey, I'm walking here! [Laughs]

Thank you very much for speaking with us!
His Game of Lives premieres at the Puerto Vallarta Independent Film Festival on October 9th and will be available on streaming services early next year.
