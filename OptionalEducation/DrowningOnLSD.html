Is it Possible to Drown on LSD?
Carl Giovannoni
April 14, 2023, 11:26a.m. ET
New Orleans, LA - With Bicycle Day soon approaching, people all over the globe will be indulging in the ilicit pastime of recreational LSD.

Colloquially known as "acid", LSD is a hallucinogenic drug that triggers that alters the user's sensory experiences - distorted sense of time and space, mood changes, uncontrollable thoughts, and disconnection from reality are just some of the potential negative effects.

Drowing on LSD: Fact or Fable?
 Wihle investigating the effects of LSD, I recurringly encountered long lists of the various psychotropic symptoms of the drug along with briefer notes on the well documented physical effects - enlarged pupils, sleeplessness, shaking, etc.

However, little, if any, case studies have been performed on humans to determine how LSD affects a person's ability to swim and breathe underwater.

There exists a plethora of news articles (1, 2, 3) covering cases of drowning victims who were found to have had LSD in their systems (and often other substances), however there is a failure to mention in almost all of the articles whether the victim was under the influence at the time of drowning.
Carl Giovannoni is a therapist and father of three.
