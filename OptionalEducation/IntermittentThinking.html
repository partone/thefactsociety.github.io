Cutting Down on Brain Use: New Fad Explained
Robert Egg
May 10, 2023, 05:46p.m. CET
GENEVA, CH - We sat down with Dr. Johannesburg Jones, a leading expert in neurology and thought pattern research at ETH Zurich, to discuss his work on Intermittent Thinking.

With extensive experience and pioneering research, Dr. Johannesburg has contributed significantly to our understanding of the human brain. In our session with him, we discussed the latest developments in his field and gained a unique perspective on the new lifestyle that is making ~brain~ waves across the youth.

Interview with Dr. Johannesburg Jones
Doctor Johannesburg Jones, image courtesy of The Fact Society.
Doctor JJ, it is a privilege to be able to talk to you today.
Dr. Johannesburg: It's a pleasure to meet you Robert! I love your work with The Fact Society, that site is truly cutting edge in the field of knowledge. [Editor's Note: That's a fact.]
Would you start us off with a brief introduction to the concept of “Intermittent Thinking?”
Of course! I think the simplest way of explaining it is that Intermittent Thinking is to the mind what Intermittent Fasting is to the gut. People are very aware of dieting fads as they can keep our bodies healthy, and maybe help us lose a few pounds. But what I've been proposing is a dieting fad for the mind, and even the ability to trim some thought fat. In extreme forms of the practice, participants have been able to reduce their thinking down to OTAD [one thought a day].
Now Doctor, when you say “thought fat” what exactly are you referring to?
As humans, we all carry around a lot of dead weight in our minds which we've labeled as “thoughts”. You see, humans were never supposed to evolve thinking. Modern science tells us that thinking is merely a side product of necessary brain functions, such as finding the nearest toilet or playing hackysack.
But tell me Doc, if we didn't think then how could we live our lives? Would we be able to talk? To dream? To love?
Well Rob, these are the very questions we are grappling with in our research. Just as intermittent thinking may reduce body fat percentage, leading to a ripped bod, so too does intermittent thinking lead to a ripped brain with the folds in our brains becoming even deeper. Unlocking powers hitherto unknown to mankind. Just the other day I swear I could read people's minds as I walked down the street since my own mind was fully deprived of thought.
Could you read my thoughts right now?
Yes, but I don't want to.
I respect that. So, Doctor, what do you think is the end result of Intermittent Thinking? It has seen widespread adoption by users of Internet, but do you think even the common man may adopt this practice?
Oh undoubtedly so. I believe it is a drive within every human to not think thoughts, and while I may have popularized this idea in modern society, it is clear that similar practices have been around for millennia all around the world. Take the caveman as an example: archaeologists have no record of their writing because they were too busy not thinking to care for the written word. And if the caveman can do it, then by God, so can modern man. I'll make sure of it!
Well Doctor, you've convinced this esteemed journalist on the benefits of intermittent thinking! Thank you for gracing us with your time tonight, and I'll think you later ;)
I'll be taking no more questions.
Doctor Johannesburg Jones disappeared from the mind's eye after this interview and has not been heard of since. We dedicate this article to you Doctor. Godspeed.
