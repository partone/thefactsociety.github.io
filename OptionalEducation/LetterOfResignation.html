My Letter of Resignation
Giane Francalich
March 20, 2026, 03:34a.m. ET
BRUSSELS, BE - Now that I have no choice but to say something about all of this, something factual; now that nothing is left to me, what I'll say is this: I reject it, all of it, in its totality and all of its totality. Before today - before this very moment, actually - I had approved of everything, and all of it. But the foot must come down somewhere, some time: this time around, this is the time, now.

It'll be said that I always expressed myself well and expressed what was sad about all of it, this, and no one will say that I didn't choose my words well when I did so. It won't protect me from the slow corrosive, still called by its old name envy, which my colleagues pamper like a pet. It'll be said that I was at the center of it and saw things from the center, and that as such I saw them one way around, but not the other, and didn't see this coming. It'll be said I was the last leg on a table to be hacked off, on a table built into the wall and with no need of walking; they'll say I was better than an abstraction, if only in that you could, using me, tell a dog to go fetch and be sure to get something back. Well! Talk about that table, then! Concrete? Not quite!

I'm no historian and won't waste my time scrying. What will they make of all this? I have some venom yet for any who'd find a scheme in me, or accuse me of secret dealings. I had two options: to rebuild myself from the sticky stuff up, or to disappear. Some here might have helped me do the latter - you've helped me this far! I've always known well where the door was! - but I couldn't trust a single one with the former. I was always going to have to do that alone.

But before I leave to Providence the task of wiping its eyes and recognizing its own angels, and wiping out the dirty remainder, I'd like to make one thing known to those who haven't known me. My mission is to destroy what I can't build and can't conceive; as such, I'm not in the habit of burning bridges. I leave that to Providence also. So, keep in touch.
Giane Francalich rejected further comment.
